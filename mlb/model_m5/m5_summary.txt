SECTION 8 — PATTERN OBSERVATIONS
======================================================================

1. Is M3 edge concentrated in specific game conditions?
   The disagreement zone analysis shows whether model divergence
   predicts edge quality. If large disagreement produces higher ROI,
   it means the lineup signal is strongest when it contradicts the
   existing team-level model — exactly when lineup composition
   deviates most from the team average.

2. Does disagreement magnitude reliably predict edge quality?
   See Section 1 buckets for monotonicity of ROI vs disagreement.

3. Does lineup deviation asymmetry explain directional bias?
   See Section 5 alignment analysis. If aligned signals (OVER+stronger
   or UNDER+weaker) outperform misaligned, then lineup deviation
   direction is a genuine signal amplifier.

4. What is the clearest next edge source after M5?
   - Forward collection of lineup release timestamps (Component 3)
   - Per-book line comparison (Component 6)
   - Live lineup-adjusted line monitoring (capture when books adjust)
   - SP scratch detection timing (early vs late scratch edge)

   M3 is validated: +7.5% ROI at edge >= 1.0 on 2024 holdout.
   M5 filters do not reliably concentrate the edge beyond M3.
   M3 edge >= 1.0 is the recommended live strategy.
