  1. WHY MECHANISMS FAILED:
     The dominant failure mode is insufficient sample size (N<50).
     The 0-1 edge zone contains only 362 discovery games total.
     Sub-filtering by ANY condition drops N below the 50-game gate
     for most hypotheses. This is not a flaw — it reflects the
     inherent narrowness of the small-edge zone.

     When N is sufficient (A2: N=108, A7: N=47),
     the ROI consistently falls BELOW the base, suggesting that
     sub-filtering doesn't improve the signal — it dilutes it.

  2. DIRECTIONAL INSTABILITY:
     Several hypotheses showed strong ROI in one season but not
     both. M10 and A5 (pace+total interactions) had promising
     combined ROI but were concentrated in one season. This is
     the hallmark of regime-dependent noise rather than structural
     market inefficiency.

  3. HIGH-PRIORITY DATA GAPS:
     Opening line data (M1, M5, M7, A3, K5) — 5 hypotheses blocked.
     This is the single most valuable data addition: line movement
     behavior is the most promising unexplored dimension for
     understanding WHEN small disagreements are more meaningful.

     Public betting % (M8) — the sharpest market behavior test.
     Would reveal whether public-side games have systematically
     different small-edge outcomes.

  4. FUTURE INTERACTION CANDIDATES:
     None from this round. No hypothesis reached PASS or NEAR-MISS.
     If opening line data becomes available, M1×A1 (line moved
     toward model + fast pace) would be the first interaction
     to test.

  5. MARKET EFFICIENCY IN THE 0-1 ZONE:
     The NBA market is remarkably efficient within the 0-1 edge
     band. The base +-13.1% ROI is diffuse — it is not
     concentrated in any game type, market condition, or team
     context. The edge appears to come from the model's average
     calibration advantage, not from any exploitable market blind
     spot.

     The already-validated fast pace overlay (A1: +27.3%) remains
     the only confirmed amplifier. It works because pace directly
     affects scoring variance — a causal mechanism — rather than
     because of a market pricing error.
