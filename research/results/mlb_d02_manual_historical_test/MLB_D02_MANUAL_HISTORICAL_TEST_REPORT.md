### What Happened

D02's mechanism predicts that rising ppbf (less efficient starters) should lead to earlier exits, more bullpen innings, and higher game totals. The data shows the opposite in discovery: flagged games had LOWER totals. The sign then flips in validation and flips back in OOS — three stages, three direction changes. This is pure noise, not signal.

### Why the Mechanism Likely Failed

The D02 discovery writeup identified the primary expected failure mode: "ppbf variation is mostly noise — driven by the opposing lineup's approach (patient teams drive up ppbf regardless of the starter's condition) rather than the starter's own deterioration." This appears to be exactly what happened. Pitches per batter faced is influenced as much by the batting team's discipline and approach as by the pitcher's efficiency, making it a noisy proxy for the intended mechanism.

Additionally, high ppbf can indicate that the starter is generating more swings-and-misses (foul balls, deep counts leading to strikeouts) rather than getting hit hard — which would suppress runs rather than increase them. The negative discovery direction is consistent with this alternative interpretation: starters with rising ppbf may actually be harder to hit, not easier.
