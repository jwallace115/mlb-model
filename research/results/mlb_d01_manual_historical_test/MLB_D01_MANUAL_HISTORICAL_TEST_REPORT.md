## 4. CANDIDATE MECHANISM CONFIRMATION

**Mechanism claim (from D01 discovery writeup):**
When the opposing starter's recent innings pitched (last 3 starts) have dropped sharply below their longer-term norm (last 10 starts), this signals a state transition: the starter is losing the ability to go deep into games. This forces an involuntary role expansion for the bullpen behind them. The hypothesis is that games where the opposing starter is in workload trajectory collapse produce more total runs than expected, because the market anchors on the starter's overall profile rather than the trajectory of their workload decline.

**Mechanism honest?** YES — the fields (`opp_sp_workload_ip_last_3`, `opp_sp_workload_ip_last_10`) directly measure what the mechanism claims: recent vs. longer-term innings per start for the opposing starter. The gap between windows is specifically a trajectory/state-transition measure, not a static quality measure.
